TempleOS, in your browser

Terry Davis's 64-bit operating system, running two completely different ways. Pick how to boot it.

Boot with QEMU

The whole PC emulated by QEMU compiled to WebAssembly (with a TempleOS-tuned HLE acceleration). Cycle-faithful x86 — boots the real desktop, full keyboard/mouse/sound, plus a built-in HolyC→WASM editor.

~13 MB engine · maximum fidelity ▶ Boot with QEMU

Boot with HEMU

A clean-room x86-64 emulator written in HolyC (~95% HolyC, <1800 lines), compiled to WASM — zero QEMU code. Resumes a live snapshot; the clock ticks and the mouse cursor & keyboard are driven by the OS itself.

~40 KB engine + 5 MB snapshot ▶ Boot with HEMU
